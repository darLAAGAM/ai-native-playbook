# Capture Privacy Hard-Stops

The abstract rule: promote business context, not personal context.

The Brain should learn from work. It should not become a surveillance archive or a dumping ground for private material.

## Never Promote

Do not promote these categories from email, Slack, Drive, meetings, or manual capture:

- HR sensitive topics.
- Recruiting, candidates, CVs, interviews, scorecards, and hiring deliberation.
- Payroll, compensation, salary bands, bonuses, equity, or personal finance details.
- Health, medical, therapy, disability, or wellbeing details.
- Family, relationship, home, or private-life context.
- Leaves, including maternity, paternity, sick leave, bereavement, or personal leave.
- Personal legal matters.
- Anything marked confidential, private, privileged, or personal.
- Full mailboxes, full chat logs, or raw private threads.

## What Can Be Promoted

Promote only the business context needed for execution:

- A decision without private backstory.
- A customer or market signal stripped of personal details.
- A deadline, owner, risk, blocker, metric, or operational process.
- A reusable learning about a tool, workflow, or system.
- A capability gap with evidence but without secrets.

## Handling Borderline Material

- If unsure, mark restricted and do not promote automatically.
- Summarize the business implication without personal details.
- Link only to authorized source paths.
- Ask the owner or privacy reviewer before converting it into a task or world model update.

## Examples

- Promote: "Contract review process needs an owner because three vendor agreements waited more than five business days."
- Do not promote: the private reason an employee was unavailable.
- Promote: "Meeting notes show the launch timeline changed to [DATE] because production readiness moved."
- Do not promote: personal details mentioned in the same meeting.
