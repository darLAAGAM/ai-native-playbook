# Employee AI Maturity L0-L3

This is an adoption framework, not a performance rating. The goal is to make AI use operational and visible.

## L0 Observer

Uses AI ad hoc or experimentally.

Concrete behaviors:

- Asks generic questions without company context.
- Does not reliably search the Brain.
- Does not know which tools exist.
- Does not write useful learnings back.

Exit criteria:

- Completes setup 1-click.
- Pastes the master prompt.
- Runs first `brain_search`.
- Uses AI on one real work task in week 1.

Expected timing: exit L0 during the first week.

## L1 Active User

Uses AI regularly with company context.

Concrete behaviors:

- Claude or approved AI client is connected to the Brain.
- Searches the Brain before asking colleagues for known context.
- Uses source systems for live data instead of guessing.
- Shares useful prompts or outputs in [AI_CHANNEL].

Exit criteria:

- Completes several real tasks with Brain evidence.
- Records at least one durable learning or output.
- Can explain privacy hard-stops.

## L2 Builder

Automates part of their own work.

Concrete behaviors:

- Turns repeated workflows into skills, prompts, templates, or simple scripts.
- Contributes useful examples to the Brain.
- Logs capability gaps with evidence.
- Helps peers use the system correctly.

Exit criteria:

- Ships one reusable skill, template, automation, or documented workflow.
- Shows measurable time saved, quality improved, or error rate reduced.
- Has at least one contribution reused by another person.

## L3 Multiplier

Creates leverage for other teams.

Concrete behaviors:

- Builds tools, workflows, or operating patterns that other teams adopt.
- Designs handoffs between agents, humans, and source systems.
- Improves health checks, onboarding, or shared world model quality.
- Coaches other employees into L1 or L2 behavior.

Exit criteria:

- Creates a reusable capability used outside their own role.
- Owns or improves a company-level AI workflow.
- Documents the pattern so it can survive without them.
