# Pricing Strategy

## Purpose

Design pricing and packaging that captures value without blocking adoption.

## Use When

Use for price changes, packaging decisions, bundles, subscriptions, tiers, discount policy, or willingness-to-pay research.

## Inputs

- Product or service offer
- Customer segments
- Current prices and conversion
- Gross margin and cost to serve
- Competitor alternatives
- Customer feedback on price

## Procedure

1. Define the value delivered and the next-best alternative.
2. Segment customers by willingness to pay, use case, and buying process.
3. Choose the pricing metric: unit, subscription, usage, tier, bundle, or service fee.
4. Map features, limits, support, and benefits into packages.
5. Check unit economics and contribution margin at each price.
6. Use research or tests to validate acceptable ranges.
7. Plan rollout, communication, grandfathering, and monitoring.

## Output

- Pricing recommendation
- Package or tier structure
- Rationale and assumptions
- Risks and customer communication notes
- Metrics to monitor

## Guardrails

- Do not price from cost alone.
- Do not use discounts that train customers to wait.
- Do not surprise existing customers without a transition plan.

## How to adapt

Customize value metrics, margin thresholds, discount policy, contract terms, and customer communication rules.
