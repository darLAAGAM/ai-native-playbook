# Competitor Creative Analysis

## Purpose

Map competitor ad strategy and identify creative white space worth testing.

## Use When

Use before creative planning, market entry, quarterly competitive reviews, or when acquisition costs rise unexpectedly.

## Inputs

- Competitor names or URLs
- Public ads, screenshots, or ad library exports
- Your current creative themes
- Target audience
- Offer and positioning

## Procedure

1. Collect active competitor ads from public sources or screenshots.
2. Categorize each ad by format, hook, message angle, offer, proof type, and CTA.
3. Estimate creative rotation by comparing active volume and launch recency.
4. Identify repeated category conventions and messaging everyone uses.
5. Identify gaps competitors are not covering.
6. Compare competitor patterns against your current creative mix.
7. Recommend tests that either exploit white space or deliberately match a proven convention.

## Output

- Competitor creative inventory
- Messaging and format map
- White-space opportunities
- Patterns worth testing
- Watchlist for future changes

## Guardrails

- Do not assume public ad presence equals performance.
- Do not copy competitor creative directly.
- Do not rely on one competitor if the category has broader patterns.

## How to adapt

Define your competitor set, prohibited claims, brand differentiation rules, and preferred creative testing cadence.
