# Copy Editing Sweep

## Purpose

Improve existing marketing copy through focused editing passes.

## Use When

Use when copy exists but needs sharper clarity, proof, specificity, voice, or conversion strength.

## Inputs

- Draft copy
- Target audience
- Desired action
- Brand voice notes
- Proof points
- Known objections

## Procedure

1. Run a clarity pass: mark anything confusing, vague, or overloaded.
2. Run a voice pass: smooth tone shifts and remove off-brand language.
3. Run a "so what" pass: connect every feature or claim to reader value.
4. Run a proof pass: flag claims that need evidence or softening.
5. Run a specificity pass: replace generic claims with concrete numbers, examples, or timeframes.
6. Run a risk pass: check CTA friction, objections, and trust signals.
7. Produce revised copy plus a change log explaining the highest-impact edits.

## Output

- Revised copy
- Issue list by sweep
- Suggested proof gaps to fill
- CTA and objection notes
- Optional before/after table

## Guardrails

- Do not rewrite the strategy unless asked.
- Do not invent proof, customer quotes, or statistics.
- Do not flatten a distinctive voice into generic professional copy.

## How to adapt

Add your brand voice examples, banned words, proof standards, legal restrictions, and preferred editing depth.
