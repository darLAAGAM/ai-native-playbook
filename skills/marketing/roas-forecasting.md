# ROAS Forecasting

## Purpose

Forecast return on ad spend across conservative, expected, and upside cases.

## Use When

Use for monthly planning, budget approvals, seasonal shifts, and leadership expectation setting.

## Inputs

- Last 30-180 days of spend and revenue
- Historical seasonality
- Planned budget changes
- Creative or campaign changes
- Inventory or capacity constraints
- Margin or payback target

## Procedure

1. Establish trailing ROAS and revenue trend.
2. Identify known drivers: seasonality, promotional calendar, creative fatigue, price changes, and audience saturation.
3. Model conservative, expected, and upside scenarios.
4. Tie each scenario to explicit assumptions.
5. Convert ROAS forecast into revenue and contribution margin impact.
6. Identify risks that could push results below target.
7. Define monitoring checkpoints and action triggers.

## Output

- 30/60/90 day forecast
- Scenario table
- Assumptions by scenario
- Revenue and margin impact
- Risks and trigger points

## Guardrails

- Do not provide one precise number without a range.
- Do not forecast beyond the data quality.
- Do not ignore conversion lag or delayed revenue recognition.

## How to adapt

Customize forecast windows, confidence bands, seasonality rules, and the financial target that makes ROAS acceptable.
