# Ad Copy Variant Generator

## Purpose

Generate new ad variants from proven patterns without losing test discipline.

## Use When

Use when strong ads are fatiguing, a campaign needs fresh angles, or a team needs testable copy variations.

## Inputs

- Top-performing ads
- Performance metrics by ad
- Audience and offer
- Platform constraints
- Brand voice rules
- Known objections

## Procedure

1. Identify the winning elements in current ads: hook, promise, proof, format, CTA, and tone.
2. Group those elements by likely driver: attention, relevance, trust, urgency, or clarity.
3. Generate variants that preserve one winning element while changing one or two others.
4. Label each variant with the variable being tested.
5. Format variants for the target platform and respect character limits.
6. Prioritize variants by expected learning value and business upside.
7. Pair each variant with a control and a measurement plan.

## Output

- Ad copy variants grouped by test angle
- Notes on preserved winning patterns
- Test priority list
- Platform-ready copy
- Recommended control/variant pairings

## Guardrails

- Do not generate random creative volume without a testable reason.
- Do not use fake claims, fake urgency, or unverifiable proof.
- Do not change too many variables if the goal is learning.

## How to adapt

Add platform rules, restricted claims, brand voice examples, offer limits, and your standard creative naming convention.
