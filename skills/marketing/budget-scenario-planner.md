# Budget Scenario Planner

## Purpose

Model what happens if marketing budget increases, decreases, or shifts across campaigns.

## Use When

Use for monthly planning, leadership asks for more spend, budget cuts, or reallocating spend after performance changes.

## Inputs

- Current spend by campaign or channel
- CPA, ROAS, revenue, and conversion volume
- Profitability targets
- Historical scaling data
- Planned changes or constraints

## Procedure

1. Establish current baseline performance by campaign and channel.
2. Separate average efficiency from marginal efficiency where data allows.
3. Identify campaigns with headroom and campaigns showing diminishing returns.
4. Build conservative, expected, and aggressive spend scenarios.
5. Estimate conversion, revenue, CPA, ROAS, and gross margin impact for each scenario.
6. Flag assumptions that drive the model: seasonality, inventory, audience size, creative freshness, and tracking quality.
7. Recommend the budget level with the best risk-adjusted return.

## Output

- Scenario table
- Recommended budget
- Expected impact range
- Key assumptions
- Risks and monitoring plan

## Guardrails

- Do not assume spend scales linearly.
- Do not recommend budget growth if operational capacity or margin cannot absorb it.
- Do not hide confidence ranges behind one precise forecast.

## How to adapt

Customize your contribution margin, payback threshold, minimum conversion volume, and channel-specific scaling constraints.
