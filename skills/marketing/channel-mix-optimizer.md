# Channel Mix Optimizer

## Purpose

Recommend the highest-return allocation of marketing budget across channels.

## Use When

Use during monthly or quarterly media planning, budget resets, and channel expansion decisions.

## Inputs

- Total available budget
- Spend and results by channel
- Marginal CPA or ROAS if available
- Funnel role of each channel
- Business objective
- Constraints by channel

## Procedure

1. Classify channels by role: demand capture, demand creation, retention, referral, or brand.
2. Calculate current and marginal efficiency by channel.
3. Identify where additional spend likely improves results and where returns are saturated.
4. Model reallocations at the same budget and at plus/minus 20 percent budget.
5. Account for lagging channels that assist conversion but do not close it directly.
6. Recommend an allocation with expected impact and confidence level.
7. Define the review window and metrics that would trigger another reallocation.

## Output

- Current vs recommended allocation
- Marginal efficiency notes
- Scenario impact
- Channel-level reasoning
- Follow-up measurement plan

## Guardrails

- Do not over-credit last-click channels without checking assisted value.
- Do not cut learning channels solely because short-term ROAS is lower.
- Do not shift budget faster than the team can monitor.

## How to adapt

Set your attribution model, minimum viable channel spend, margin target, and strategic channels that need protected test budgets.
