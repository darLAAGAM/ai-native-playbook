# Humanize AI Copy

## Purpose

Remove generic AI phrasing and restore direct, specific, human-sounding copy.

## Use When

Use when copy feels padded, robotic, over-polished, generic, or full of AI cliches.

## Inputs

- Draft text
- Intended reader
- Desired tone
- Specific facts or examples
- Words or phrases to avoid

## Procedure

1. Mark overused transitions, hedging, filler, and corporate jargon.
2. Replace abstract claims with concrete facts or examples.
3. Shorten sentences that announce what they are about to say.
4. Vary rhythm with a mix of short and medium-length sentences.
5. Remove empty intensifiers and generic adjectives.
6. Preserve the useful argument while making the voice more direct.
7. List places where the writer must add real specifics.

## Output

- Humanized draft
- Removed phrases list
- Notes on missing specifics
- Optional style rules for future drafts

## Guardrails

- Do not make the copy casual if the brand requires restraint.
- Do not remove necessary legal or technical precision.
- Do not add personality at the cost of clarity.

## How to adapt

Create a brand-specific replacement list, examples of good voice, and a banned-phrase library.
