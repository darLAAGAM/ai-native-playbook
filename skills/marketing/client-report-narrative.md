# Client Report Narrative

## Purpose

Turn raw performance metrics into a clear executive narrative.

## Use When

Use for weekly or monthly reports, stakeholder updates, agency client notes, and board-ready summaries.

## Inputs

- Reporting period and comparison period
- Core metrics
- Biggest wins and declines
- Known changes during the period
- Next actions
- Audience and tone

## Procedure

1. Identify the one most important thing that happened.
2. State the result with numbers and comparison.
3. Explain the likely driver using evidence, not vibes.
4. Mention the biggest risk or watch item.
5. Tie next actions directly to the data.
6. Remove platform jargon unless the audience uses it.
7. Keep the narrative short enough to be read first.

## Output

- Executive summary paragraph
- Key wins
- Watch items
- Next actions
- Optional short version for Slack or email

## Guardrails

- Do not over-explain every metric.
- Do not hide bad news behind vague wording.
- Do not claim causality unless the evidence supports it.

## How to adapt

Define report tone, required metrics, reporting cadence, and the decisions each report should support.
