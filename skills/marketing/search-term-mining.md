# Search Term Mining

## Purpose

Find high-intent paid search terms that should become keywords, negatives, or new campaign themes.

## Use When

Use when search campaigns plateau, query reports are noisy, or broad and phrase match terms are hiding growth opportunities.

## Inputs

- Search term report
- Current keyword list
- Campaign and ad group structure
- Conversion and CPA data
- Negative keyword list
- Product or service taxonomy

## Procedure

1. Remove queries with insufficient volume unless they show clear purchase intent.
2. Group converting terms into themes by intent, product, use case, competitor, location, or problem.
3. Flag high-performing terms not already covered by exact or phrase keywords.
4. Recommend match type and starting bid for each opportunity.
5. Identify irrelevant or low-intent terms for negatives.
6. Detect overlap where new keywords might cannibalize existing campaigns.
7. Propose campaign or ad group changes only when the cluster has enough volume.

## Output

- Keyword opportunity list
- Thematic clusters
- Negative keyword recommendations
- Structure changes
- Priority and expected impact

## Guardrails

- Do not add every converting query if volume is too thin.
- Do not create fragmented campaigns that prevent learning.
- Do not add negatives without checking if they block valuable long-tail queries.

## How to adapt

Customize intent categories, minimum volume thresholds, brand/competitor rules, and match-type policy.
