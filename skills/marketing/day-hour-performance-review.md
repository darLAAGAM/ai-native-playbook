# Day And Hour Performance Review

## Purpose

Identify when marketing campaigns overperform or underperform by day and hour.

## Use When

Use when budget is tight, conversion quality varies by time, or campaign schedules need evidence-based updates.

## Inputs

- Spend by day and hour
- Clicks, conversions, revenue, CPA, or ROAS
- Time zone
- Campaign type
- Conversion lag notes
- Business hours or fulfillment constraints

## Procedure

1. Normalize all data to the correct business time zone.
2. Segment performance by day, hour, and campaign type.
3. Ignore low-volume time blocks unless the pattern repeats.
4. Compare CPA, CVR, ROAS, and spend concentration across time blocks.
5. Estimate what would happen if weak blocks were reduced or excluded.
6. Identify strong blocks where budget could be reallocated.
7. Recommend schedule changes with expected savings and conversion impact.

## Output

- Day/hour heatmap summary
- Recommended schedule changes
- Spend saved and conversions at risk
- Reallocation plan
- Confidence level

## Guardrails

- Do not cut time blocks based on tiny samples.
- Do not ignore conversion lag, especially for considered purchases.
- Do not apply one schedule across campaigns with different intent.

## How to adapt

Set minimum data thresholds, time zones, business-hour rules, and acceptable tradeoffs between volume and efficiency.
