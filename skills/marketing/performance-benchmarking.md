# Performance Benchmarking

## Purpose

Compare marketing performance against realistic internal or external benchmarks.

## Use When

Use in monthly reviews, stakeholder reporting, audits, or when a team asks whether a metric is good.

## Inputs

- Current metrics
- Segment, channel, campaign type, or market
- Historical performance
- External benchmark source if available
- Business model and funnel stage

## Procedure

1. Define the metric, scope, and comparison period.
2. Choose the right benchmark: internal history, peer accounts, industry data, or target economics.
3. Adjust interpretation for funnel stage, geography, budget size, and account maturity.
4. Compare variance from benchmark in percentage and absolute terms.
5. Separate symptoms from likely causes.
6. Prioritize gaps by business impact, not embarrassment.
7. Recommend what to inspect or test next.

## Output

- Benchmark comparison table
- Outperforming and underperforming areas
- Likely root causes
- Priority opportunities
- Confidence level and caveats

## Guardrails

- Do not compare unlike metrics or funnel stages.
- Do not use stale benchmarks without saying so.
- Do not treat averages as targets if unit economics disagree.

## How to adapt

Maintain your own benchmark library by channel, market, customer type, product category, and campaign objective.
