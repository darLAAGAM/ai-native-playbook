# Signup Flow CRO

## Purpose

Reduce signup friction and increase account or trial completion.

## Use When

Use for account creation, trial starts, waitlists, registration, checkout account creation, or freemium signup flows.

## Inputs

- Signup steps and fields
- Completion rate and drop-off by step
- Required data and why it is needed
- Authentication options
- Post-signup destination
- Compliance or verification needs

## Procedure

1. Define the minimum information needed before the user can get value.
2. Separate signup friction from onboarding questions that can happen later.
3. Review field count, social auth, password UX, validation, and error states.
4. Check whether the value promise is clear before commitment.
5. Improve mobile layout and autofill behavior.
6. Map the immediate post-submit state and next action.
7. Propose quick wins and A/B tests.

## Output

- Signup friction audit
- Recommended step and field structure
- Copy for CTAs, labels, errors, and success state
- Test backlog
- Measurement plan

## Guardrails

- Do not defer data that is legally or operationally required.
- Do not force account creation when guest or low-friction alternatives fit the business.
- Do not optimize signups if activation quality collapses.

## How to adapt

Customize required fields, authentication policy, verification rules, and your definition of a qualified signup.
