# Form CRO

## Purpose

Improve form completion without collecting less data than the business needs.

## Use When

Use for lead forms, contact forms, quote requests, surveys, application forms, checkout forms, and demo forms.

## Inputs

- Form type and goal
- Current fields and required fields
- Completion and drop-off data
- Mobile/desktop split
- Business use for each field
- Compliance requirements

## Procedure

1. Identify the form's job and what happens after submission.
2. Challenge every field: required now, optional, infer later, or remove.
3. Reorder fields from easiest to most sensitive.
4. Improve labels, help text, placeholders, and error messages.
5. Check mobile usability, autofill, keyboard types, and tap targets.
6. Add trust or expectation copy only where it reduces a real objection.
7. Create quick fixes and test hypotheses.

## Output

- Field-by-field audit
- Recommended field set and order
- Copy for labels, errors, and CTA
- Mobile notes
- Test hypotheses

## Guardrails

- Do not remove fields required for compliance or fulfillment.
- Do not optimize completion rate while destroying lead quality.
- Do not use manipulative decline or consent language.

## How to adapt

Define your required data, lead quality threshold, compliance text, and acceptable tradeoff between conversion and qualification.
