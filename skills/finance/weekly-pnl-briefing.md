# Weekly P&L Briefing

## Purpose

Create an operator-grade weekly P&L readout that separates signal from noise.

## Use When

Use this skill when an operator needs a repeatable procedure for create an operator-grade weekly P&L readout that separates signal from noise.

## Inputs

- Current week sales
- COGS or gross margin
- Ad spend
- Payroll and opex
- Previous week and budget

## Procedure

1. Calculate revenue, gross margin, contribution margin, fixed opex, EBITDA proxy, cash movement, and variance to plan.
2. Compare to previous week, trailing 4-week average, and budget.
3. Identify the 3-5 drivers that explain the variance in money terms.
4. Flag anomalies: margin compression, spend spike, refund spike, stockout, payroll mismatch, late invoices, or revenue recognition issue.
5. Recommend actions with owner and deadline.

## Output

- Executive summary
- KPI table
- Variance drivers
- Risks
- Actions

## Guardrails

- Do not hide cash issues behind revenue growth.
- Label estimates clearly.
- Do not mix booked sales, cash receipts, and accounting revenue without noting basis.

## How to adapt

Adjust metric definitions for your accounting basis, channel mix, and margin structure.
