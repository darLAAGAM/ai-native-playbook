# Launch Plan

## Purpose

Plan a launch that builds momentum before, during, and after launch day.

## Use When

Use for new products, services, features, offers, markets, stores, waitlists, beta programs, and major announcements.

## Inputs

- What is launching
- Target audience
- Launch date or timeline
- Owned, rented, and borrowed channels
- Assets available
- Success metrics
- Constraints and risks

## Procedure

1. Define the launch objective and audience.
2. Split the launch into phases: internal, alpha, beta, early access, full launch, and post-launch if relevant.
3. Map owned channels, rented channels, and borrowed channels.
4. Prepare assets: landing page, email, announcement, social posts, FAQs, demos, and support notes.
5. Set launch-day roles, monitoring, and response plan.
6. Plan post-launch follow-up, onboarding, feedback capture, and second-wave content.
7. Define success metrics by phase and a retrospective date.

## Output

- Launch timeline
- Channel plan
- Asset checklist
- Launch-day runbook
- Post-launch plan
- Success metrics

## Guardrails

- Do not treat launch day as the whole launch.
- Do not announce before the offer and support flow are ready.
- Do not rely only on rented platforms without capturing owned audience.

## How to adapt

Customize phase names, approval workflow, channel mix, asset templates, and the metrics that define launch success.
