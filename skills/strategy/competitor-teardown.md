# Competitor Teardown

## Purpose

Analyze a competitor’s positioning, offer, pricing, funnel, and likely strategic intent.

## Use When

Use when you need to analyze a competitor’s positioning, offer, pricing, funnel, and likely strategic intent.

## Inputs

- Competitor URLs
- Screenshots or ad examples
- Pricing pages
- Your positioning/context

## Procedure

1. Capture the competitor’s promise, target customer, product categories, offers, and proof.
2. Map funnel: acquisition channel, landing page, email capture, checkout/demo, retention hooks.
3. Compare pricing, packaging, promotions, and guarantees.
4. Identify strengths, weaknesses, copy angles, and operational implications.
5. Recommend what to copy, ignore, counter-position, or test.

## Output

- Competitor profile
- Positioning map
- Funnel notes
- Opportunities
- Threats

## Guardrails

- Do not assume their strategy is working without evidence.
- Avoid copying claims you cannot substantiate.

## How to adapt

Adapt teardown dimensions to ecommerce, services, marketplace, or local retail.
